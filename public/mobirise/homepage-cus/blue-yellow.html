Blue-yellow Colour Blind

People affected by blue-yellow colour blind confuse blue with green and yellow with violet. So the term blue-green colour blind would be more accurate because the colours blue and yellow are usually not mixed up.

Although rarer than red-green color vision deficiency, blue-yellow colour deficiency is actually equally present in both males and females. The gene coding for the blue receptor lies on chromosome 7, which is shared by both males and females, hence why both sexes are equally affected. Blue colour blind is caused by a mutation in this gene, and it results in two different conditions.

What are the four types of red-green colour blind?

This less-common type of color vision deficiency makes it hard to tell the difference between several different color combinations.

There are 2 types of blue-yellow color vision deficiency:

1. Tritanomaly 
Tritanomaly indicates limited or defective blue cone cells. Blue appears green-ish and distinguishing yellow or red from pink is neigh-on impossible. Tritanomaly is the rarest of all color blindness conditions, affecting well under 0.01% of both males and females. 

2. Tritanopia 
People who experience Tritanopia are lacking in blue cone cells. Blue appears identical to green and yellow is easily mixed up with violet or even dark grey. As with Tritanomaly, Tritanopia affects both sexes, but itâ€™s much more common. Around 1% of the population experiences it.

What does blue-yellow colour blind look like?

It is a bit difficult to imagine a colour deficiency if you have normal vision. People who can not see blue or yellow might perceive things the rest of us would think with some blue and yellow tones. People with blue-yellow colour blind also have difficulty making out the differences between pale shades. 

Blue-yellow colour blind people often struggle to differentiate between pale or pastel colors, especially those that fall within the blue or yellow spectrum. These colours can appear more similar to each other than they do for people with normal colour vision.
